10 Things to Declutter Right Now for Instant Calm

March 15, 2026 Emma Richardson 5 min read

Feeling overwhelmed by the stuff in your home? You're not alone. Studies show that clutter increases cortisol levels and reduces our ability to focus. The good news? You don't need a full weekend to make a difference.

Start Small, Feel Big

The key to successful decluttering is starting with quick wins. These small changes create momentum and motivation for bigger projects down the road.

"A clutter-free space is a clutter-free mind. Start with one drawer, and the rest will follow." — Emma Richardson, SpaceCraft Founder

The 10 Items to Tackle Today

Here's our curated list of items that have the biggest impact when decluttered:

1. Expired Products

Check your medicine cabinet, pantry, and fridge. Expired items take up valuable space and can be harmful. Set a calendar reminder to do this monthly.

2. Duplicate Kitchen Utensils

Do you really need four spatulas? Keep the best version of each tool and donate or recycle the rest.

3. Unread Magazines & Newspapers

If you haven't read it in a week, you won't. Recycle the pile and switch to digital subscriptions.

4. Old Chargers & Cables

That tangled drawer of mystery cables? Most of them belong to devices you no longer own.

5. Clothes You Haven't Worn in a Year

The "one-year rule" works. If it hasn't left the hanger in 12 months, it's time to let it go.

Remember, decluttering isn't about deprivation — it's about making room for what truly matters. Start with these five and see how you feel. We bet you'll want to keep going.
